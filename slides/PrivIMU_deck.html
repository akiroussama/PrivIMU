Act I — Context

IMU sensors are everywhere in IoT

Smartphones
DeviceMotion API on every iOS / Android phone. Default-enabled by web pages without prompt.
Wearables
Smartwatches, fitness bands, sleep trackers. Continuous accelerometer + gyroscope at 50–200 Hz.
Smart mobility
Connected vehicles, e-scooters, drones. Inertial signals shared with telemetry back-ends.
Health & sport
Gait monitoring, fall detection, rehab apps, AR/VR headsets. Often forwarded to third-party clouds.
Why this slide matters

IMU streams are perceived as low-risk because they don't carry a name, a face or a voice. They are routinely collected, stored, shared with analytics SDKs, and treated as "anonymous telemetry". PrivIMU asks a simple question : is that perception correct ?

Act I — Question

Anonymous ≠ non-identifiable

The scientific question

Can a supervised classifier trained on accelerometer + gyroscope windows re-identify a user among 24 enrolled candidates, when the input contains no direct identifier (no name, email, MAC, IMEI) ?

The problem is not the explicit identifier. The problem is the latent behavioural signature hidden in motion patterns — gait, posture micro-tremor, hand dynamics.

random baseline

Hypothesis

1-second windows of 6 IMU channels (rotationRate.{x,y,z} + userAcceleration.{x,y,z}) carry enough behavioural information to be a quasi-identifier in a closed-set setting. The next slides design the threat model that operationalises that hypothesis.

Ethical scope

Act II — Positioning

From activity recognition to identity leakage

Human Activity Recognition

Anguita 2013 (UCI HAR), Malekzadeh 2018 (MotionSense). Same channels, different label : the activity, not the user.

Behavioural biometrics

Gait recognition (Gafurov 2007, Derawi 2010), keystroke dynamics, smartphone-touch biometrics. Identity is the goal, often with cooperative protocols.

Sensor privacy leakage

Mehrnezhad 2018 (touch via motion), Das 2016 (accelerometer fingerprinting), AccessMine 2019. Demonstrate that sensors leak more than expected.

Where PrivIMU sits

HAR + biometrics + leakage, repackaged as a reproducible privacy lab with attack & defence on the same dataset.

Defences in the literature

Data minimisation, on-device computation, differential privacy, federated learning, additive noise, feature-level sharing. PrivIMU prototypes one (Gaussian noise) and discusses the rest as a privacy-by-design checklist.

Act III — Method

How the signal becomes evidence

Windowing
  • 1 second = 50 samples
  • 50% overlap = step 25
  • z-score per window per channel
  • final dataset : 55 982 windows
Features (60 per window)

For each of 6 channels, 10 statistics :

  • mean, std, rms, min, max, median
  • iqr, skew, kurtosis
  • spectral entropy
Models
  • Random Forest (primary) — 200 trees, balanced subsample.
  • 1D-CNN (extension) — raw windows, 3 conv blocks, in model_cnn.py.
  • Both trained with GroupKFold over activity_trial.
Why GroupKFold matters

Naive K-fold splits randomly across windows of the same trial → data-leakage between train and test → over-optimistic top-1. PrivIMU groups by (subject, activity, trial) and forces folds to never split a trial. This is what makes our 39% top-1 honest.

Act III — Evaluation

Evaluation : avoid inflated results

Metrics, all generated by code
  • Top-1 accuracy — right subject ranked first.
  • Top-3 accuracy — right subject in top 3.
  • Macro-F1 — balanced across 24 subjects.
  • Confusion matrix — visual diagnostic.
  • Per-subject F1 — spot vulnerable users.
  • Posterior entropy H(p) — uncertainty (bits).
  • Privacy leakage ΔH = log2(24) − H(p).
  • Latency ms / window — deployability.
Cross-validation strategy

GroupKFold(n_splits=5, group_key="activity_trial")

Each of the 5 folds holds out a subset of trials. The model is trained on the remaining trials and predicts on out-of-fold windows. We aggregate out-of-fold posteriors over the full 55 982 windows.

Honesty rule

If our split is not Leave-One-Trial-Out, we don't claim it. The deck and report state exactly what train.py does : 5-fold GroupKFold by activity-trial.

Act IV — Defence

Blue team : how loud must we be to hide ?

Defence model

Inject Gaussian noise x' = x + ε, ε ∼ ℕ(0, σ2), before any feature is computed. Sweep σ and observe the trade-off between privacy gain (lower top-1, higher posterior entropy) and utility loss (signal-preservation proxy on raw channels).

Trade-off curve (illustrative)
Take-away

Gaussian noise is a pedagogical baseline, not a guarantee. The right defence stack mixes data minimisation (reduce sample rate, drop channels), on-device computation (don't send raw windows), and differential privacy (calibrated noise on aggregates). PrivIMU shows the shape of that trade-off live in the demo.

Act V — Limits

Limits & privacy-by-design mitigations

What this study does not claim
  • 24 subjects — not population-scale.
  • Closed-set identification — new subjects not modelled.
  • Single device class — iPhone 6s, phone-in-pocket.
  • Single dataset — MotionSense, controlled lab.
  • No adversarial robustness — attacker uses the same feature space.
  • Gaussian noise defence is illustrative, not certified DP.
Mitigations — a privacy-by-design checklist
  • Data minimisation — collect only the channels and rate the use-case requires.
  • On-device inference — never ship raw IMU windows to the cloud.
  • Federated learning — train without centralising raw traces.
  • Differential privacy — calibrated noise on shared aggregates.
  • Feature-level sharing — release coarse features, not raw signals.
  • Consent & transparency — treat IMU as PII in data notices.
A defensible posture

A 39% top-1 on 24 subjects is not a real-world identification system. It is a proof that the signal carries identity. Under more subjects, more sessions, deeper models or device-fingerprint cross-correlation, the number can only grow. The right reaction is to treat motion data as personal data — not to wait for the attack at scale.
