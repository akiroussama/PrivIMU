# PrivIMU

## Motion Anonymity Attack on IoT Sensor Data

**Question:** can anonymous accelerometer + gyroscope traces reveal user identity?

---

# 1. IoT sensors are everywhere

- Smartphones
- Wearables
- Health trackers
- Smart mobility
- Sport applications

Motion traces are often treated as low-risk because they do not contain direct identifiers.

---

# 2. The privacy gap

No name. No email. No face.  
But a movement pattern can still be distinctive.

**Hypothesis:** IMU data can act as a behavioral quasi-identifier.

---

# 3. Threat model

An attacker has:

- public training data or prior enrolled traces;
- accelerometer + gyroscope windows;
- a modest ML classifier.

The attacker predicts the most likely subject among 24 enrolled candidates.

---

# 4. Related work

Three research lines meet here:

1. Human Activity Recognition
2. Sensor-data privacy
3. Behavioral biometrics

---

# 5. Our contribution

PrivIMU is a reproducible mini-lab:

- data download;
- preprocessing;
- ML training;
- privacy metrics;
- live Streamlit demo;
- mitigation slider.

---

# 6. Dataset

**MotionSense**

- 24 subjects
- 6 activities
- iPhone in pocket
- 50 Hz sampling
- accelerometer + gyroscope-derived signals

---

# 7. Preprocessing

- 1-second windows
- 50% overlap
- per-window z-score
- six IMU channels
- 60 features per window

---

# 8. Models

Primary model:

- Random Forest
- fast on CPU
- interpretable baseline

Extension:

- 1D-CNN on raw windows

---

# 11. Live attack

The model receives a signal window and returns a posterior distribution over 24 subjects.

The key moment: the signal has no explicit identity field.

---

# 12. Defense slider

We add Gaussian noise to the signal.

Expected visual effect:

- lower confidence;
- higher posterior entropy;
- lower leakage score.

# 14. Limits and mitigations

Limits:

- 24 subjects only;
- closed-set identification;
- phone-in-pocket setting.

Mitigations:

- data minimization;
- edge inference;
- differential privacy;
- federated learning.

---

# 15. Takeaway

Anonymous does not always mean non-identifiable.

**PrivIMU shows that IoT motion data should be treated as privacy-sensitive.**
